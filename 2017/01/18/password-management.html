Password management

Jan 18, 2017

How to choose a good password. Each time I read something like that I remember a great comic stripe at xkcd.com.

Password’s strength is not about how difficult to remember is. It’s about how difficult to guess is, either by another human or by computer programs. So instead of joining the initial letters of a common saying, changing cases and adding some numbers, it would be wiser to use that common saying plus anything you want. The longer the password is, the better.

For example, there’s a common Catalan saying …

If you do not like the dust, do not go to the threshing floor

I am sure there are better ways to translate Si no vols pols, no vagis a l’era Do not shorten it! Why would you choose Iydnltddngtttf   if you can choose the whole sentence? Well, in English the shortened version is 14 characters long, which is pretty good, but not as good as the 61 characters long full version!

There’s a problem here, of course. Some sites force the password to be less than 8 characters long or whatever. Even worse, there are sites which still do not hash the passwords - see next paragraph - so if the site is hacked, the plain password will be there… You may check if a given site stores the passwords as plain text using the site’s forgot password procedure: if the service emails you your real password, all your efforts choosing such secure password were worthless. You spent your time to choose a secure (and long) password just to realize the related web service stores the password in plain format. If the site is hacked and its user database dumped, the hackers will get all its passwords.

Username password
john_doe yummycookies
jane_doe 123456
mick_doe passw0rd

The solution is hashing. If the reader does not know what hash means, let’s say it is a mathematical transformation of the password into a fixed-length meaningless string. That transformation can be applied to just the password (non-salted hash) or to the concatenation of the password and a random word (salted hash). Such transformation can not be reversed, so to guess a hashed password, there are two options: a dictionary attack (try words fetched from a file) or just brute-force (check all combinations for any given length, then try another length and so on, until hashed combination matches password hash).

Username password_md5
john_doe 437f8d04bb44a673768f5ee30e8105b9
jane_doe e10adc3949ba59abbe56e057f20f883e
mick_doe bed128365216c019988915ed3add75fb 
$ john --format=raw-md5 passwordsmd5.txt 
Loaded 3 password hashes with no different salts (Raw MD5 [SSE2 32x4])
123456           (?)
passw0rd         (?)
guesses: 2  time: 0:00:00:11 0.00% (3)  c/s: 2869K  trying: dbbl18 - dbdg21

Simple passwords hashed with a fast/weak hashing algorithm (md5) are guessed in just a few seconds.

john_doe 0fdcf0e43cf0ececec55e…
jane_doe ba3253876aed6bc22d4a6…
mick_doe e0469addd8d57a3623494… 
$ john --format=raw-sha512 passwords512.txt

123456           (?)
passw0rd         (?)
guesses: 2  time: 0:00:00:03 0.00% (3)  c/s: 66552  trying: sammm

Even when hashed with strong hash functions (sha512), simple passwords are guessed also in no time, as you may see above. Download john the ripper here.

If passwords have been salted, then a given dictionary is salted and hashed and then cross checked with the passwords. The difficulty here is to find out the right place of the salt.

$ cat rockyou.txt | php -r 'while($f=fgets(STDIN)){echo hash("sha512","salted".rtrim($f)).": ".$f;};' > salted_rockyou.txt
$ grep d55228f2296db688eff1bd7...47ee1d9d4eabe7352f2b60677 salted_rockyou.txt 
d55228f2296db688eff1bd7...47ee1d9d4eabe7352f2b60677: yummycookies

You may have heard about password leakages. Now you may realize there is a huge difference between a leakage of 2 million salted-hash passwords and a leakage of 2 million plain passwords. Hashing passwords make things more difficult for password crackers, and very long passwords not appearing in common dictionaries will take even years to crack with huge hardware.

Yes, major players of the Internet use salted hashes. Moreover, the two step authentication is gaining momentum because many top players are implementing it. Great news indeed!
